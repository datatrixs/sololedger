The Myth of Passive Income & The Power of Data

Serge Amouzou

August 27, 2025 · 5 min read

The idea of "passive income" is often a siren's call in the creator economy. But as our panelists from the inaugural Verza event revealed, a thriving creative business is anything but passive. It's built on strategy, hard work, and, most importantly, data.

The panel, featuring Juliana and Martin from @TouristToLocal and creator Stephanie Chiu (@ByStephanieChiu), offered a candid look into what it takes to build a resilient and profitable business.

The Myth of "Set It and Forget It"

Stephanie Chiu, a content creator and software engineer, was blunt about the misconception of passive income. She explained that even for a mini-course or digital template, the work is far from over once the product is built.

"There's a lot of work that goes behind it," she said, "not just the fact that you have to build out that product, but then you have to figure out how to market, how to sell it."

Her data-driven approach, a skill she attributes to her engineering background, showed that her four revenue streams were not equal. Her coaching program brought in the most, while digital goods were a smaller, though still valuable, piece of the puzzle. This understanding allowed her to focus her efforts where they mattered most.

Diversification is Key to Resilience

For Juliana and Martin of TouristToLocal, their "aha moment" came when their creator income started to rival their regular jobs. They realized that waiting for ad revenue alone wasn't a sustainable strategy.

"We thought like one day we'd wake up and AdSense would be high and we would be able to do it," Juliana shared. "And like that day is probably not gonna come anytime soon."

Instead, they began to diversify, experimenting with affiliate marketing, a travel blog, and even group trips. They found that these smaller revenue streams, when combined, created a more resilient business. As Martin put it:

"It's just like a lot of little things coming in to add up to something bigger."

The Creator's Playbook: Act Like a Business

The core lesson from the panel was clear: treat your creative work like a business from day one. This means:

Tracking Your Metrics

Know where your revenue is coming from and what's driving your growth.

Investing in Yourself

Learn about pricing, contracts, and business strategy.

Embracing Consistency

Success takes time. As Stephanie noted, consistency is one of the most critical factors, and "a lot of people are not willing to put in" the work.

Data-Driven Decisions

Use analytics to understand what's working and optimize your strategy accordingly.

The Future of Creator Business

As the creator economy continues to evolve, the most successful creators won't just be the most talented—they'll be the ones who are also the most strategic.

The panel's insights revealed that passive income is a myth that distracts creators from building sustainable businesses. Instead, focus on creating multiple revenue streams, understanding your data, and treating your creative work with the same rigor as any other business venture.
