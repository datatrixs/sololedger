The Power of Authenticity: Why Your Flaws are Your Greatest Asset

Verza Team

August 28, 2025 · 6 min read

In a world of highly-polished content and meticulously curated feeds, how do you stand out? The answer, according to our panelists, isn't perfection—it's authenticity.

During Verza's "The Art & Business of Creating" panel, Juliana and Martin of @TouristToLocal and Stephanie Chiu (@ByStephanieChiu) shared how being genuine, raw, and even vulnerable was the key to building a loyal and engaged community.

Don't Be a Travel Guide, Be a Storyteller

Juliana and Martin's travel content is about more than just showing off beautiful destinations. It's about connecting with their audience on a deeper, more human level.

"We are not experts in every place that we visit," Martin explained. "But we are experts on showing how we feel about the place."

Juliana added that their content became more resonant when they started sharing the raw, behind-the-scenes struggles of their own lives, such as Martin's immigration journey. This vulnerability turned their channel from a simple travel guide into a personal story that their audience could connect with.

"If you are a traveler who is terrified of flights and flying, like, own that," Juliana said. "You don't need to hide that... People will connect with it. When you start to show your flaws, people will actually be more drawn to you."

The Rise of the Raw & Unpolished

Stephanie Chiu echoed this sentiment from a different perspective. After a rebrand that she described as "rough," she found that a more authentic approach felt better and resonated more with her audience. She noted that this shift towards unpolished content is a major trend, especially among Gen Z creators who are not afraid to share their vulnerabilities and emotions on camera.

"You have to deal with that," Martin shared about negative comments. "I try, whenever I have one, I try to focus on the 98% of the positive comments on that." Juliana even revealed that they have a "wall of inspiration" where they print out and display nice comments to counteract the negativity. This kind of vulnerability and mental health awareness is part of the authentic conversation that builds community.

Finding Your North Star

So how do you stay true to yourself when brand deals and monetization are on the line? All the panelists agreed that having a clear sense of your core values is essential.

"I always revert back to what are my values and what's like my mission behind the content that I create," Stephanie said.

Juliana and Martin even created a formal "brand guide" with their mission and values so they have a document to fall back on when a brand collaboration doesn't align with their vision.

Authenticity: Your Greatest Asset

In an industry built on personal connection, authenticity isn't just a buzzword—it's your greatest asset and the foundation of a lasting career.

The panel's insights revealed that trying to be perfect or overly polished can actually be counterproductive. Instead, embracing your flaws, sharing your struggles, and staying true to your values creates the kind of deep connection that builds loyal audiences and sustainable careers.
